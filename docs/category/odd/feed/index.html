The couple lies in the middle of the street to take their wedding photos. Photo: screenshot of video posted by Knews

Eschewing the traditional romantic seaside or flowery background, a couple in Central China took their wedding photos in the middle of zebra crossing of a busy intersection before being chased away by the traffic police.

A traffic surveillance video posted by Knews on Sina Weibo shows several staff of the photography company in Hubei Province laying out a red blanket in the left turn lane of the busy intersection, completely ignoring passing and oncoming traffic and pedestrians.

The bride in her white wedding dress and the groom in his black suit walk into the flow of traffic as the photographer snaps their photo. They even pose sitting and lying on a blanket in the middle of the road. A number of pedestrians were confused by the scene and passing cars had to slow down to avoid hitting them and each other.

After being alerted to the near mayhem by passers-by, traffic police finally shooed the couple away. The report did not mention if the couple and photographer were fined for their actions.

Wang Zhenxi is an extremely busy support therapist who says she counsels more than 800 mistresses a year and even helps them find true, monogamous love.

Wang says she uses four mobile phones to take calls from women seeking her help. “I meet clients 330 days a year,” said Wang in a seven-minute video posted by the Zhengzhou Evening News that features a lot of tears.

The soft-spoken Wang says she first befriends her clients who include brokenhearted mistresses and angry spouses whose partners have cheated on them. She will even introduce her clients to new lovers and help them start a new life.

In the video, Wang is seen paying a visit to a 27-year-old woman who had been in a relationship with a man 15 years her senior. She had just had an abortion and her paramour had disappeared from her life. 

“I did not know he was married and has a child until his wife came looking for me,” she said.

“That’s when I decided to break up with him but he got down on his knees and begged me not to leave him, saying that he didn’t love his wife. I’m softhearted and ended up staying with him,” said the woman through teas

Wang has introduced the woman to a man who has promised to marry her and the couple are seen sitting together holding hands.

Many Chinese netizens consider this profession as necessary and beneficial.

“It’s a good job, which can help not only a family but also mistresses who don’t find a correct way,” one of Sina Weibo users commented.

]]> https://oxnews.net/article/789.html/feed 0 Woman compensated for dentist’s errors https://oxnews.net/article/790.html https://oxnews.net/article/790.html#respond Thu, 21 Nov 2019 09:48:40 +0000 https://oxnews.net/article/790.html



Photo: IC

A series of unfortunate and uncomfortable events involving a woman’s entry and exit points for food resulted in her getting 12,000 yuan ($1,703) in compensation from her dentist. 

The woman in Northeast China said she had botched dental surgery which caused her extreme case of hemorrhoids, for which she had surgery four years ago, to once again rear their ugly head – so to speak.

The woman surnamed Chen said her first treatment at a dentist in Harbin, Heilongjiang Province, only exacerbated problems with her teeth.

Chen told Knews her dentist damaged her gums during her first visit and had to have additional surgery to replace a tooth. During that visit the replacement tooth popped loose and she swallowed it. 

In a panic, Chen took a powerful laxative that was utterly effective over the next four days.  

“I needed to go to toilet seven or eight times a day, which led to my hemorrhoid returning,” Chen said. 

The series of blunders by the dentist enraged Chen and she demanded compensation but is still not satisfied with the 12,000 yuan payout she received.

Chen’s tooth was finally repaired at another dental clinic, according to the report of Knews.

“Actually swallowing a broken tooth is no big deal, it will reappear at the other end in a few days,”a netizen wrote.

Let sleeping bears lie? Not in Poland this New Year’s Eve, where a row is brewing over the decision to relocate an annual televised event with fireworks and partying to the slopes of the country’s main ski resort.

By Tuesday more than 27,000 people had signed a petition organized by a local newspaper, which broke the story after discovering the plan at a council meeting in late October.

Hundreds of protesters walked across the city of Zakopane with a fake bear on a stretcher on Saturday, days after the mayor confirmed the relocation, calling on him to stop the process.

TVP public television station, which organizes the show, criticized what it called “pseudo-ecologists” and argued that sports events had been held in the area in the past without any problems.

An expert from the park confirmed to AFP that there were Carpathian brown bears in the area, though he said precise numbers were not known.
